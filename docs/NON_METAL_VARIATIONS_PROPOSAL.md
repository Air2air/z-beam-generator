# Non-Metal Material Variations Proposal

**Version**: 1.0.0  
**Date**: November 7, 2025  
**Purpose**: Define variations for non-metal materials (wood, stone, ceramic, glass, composite, plastic, etc.)

---

## Overview

Unlike metals which have alloys, non-metal materials have **grades, varieties, compositions, and treatments** that significantly affect their properties and laser cleaning behavior. This document proposes variations for each non-metal category.

---

## Wood Materials

### Variation Types
- **Species variations** (different wood types)
- **Cut variations** (quarter-sawn, flat-sawn, rift-sawn)
- **Moisture content** (green, air-dried, kiln-dried)
- **Age/condition** (new, aged, weathered, historic)
- **Treatment** (untreated, sealed, stained, painted)

### Oak Variations
- **White Oak** (Quercus alba)
  - Density: 0.68-0.75 g/cmÂ³
  - Applications: Furniture, flooring, barrels
  - Laser cleaning: Moderate power, watch for darkening
  
- **Red Oak** (Quercus rubra)
  - Density: 0.63-0.70 g/cmÂ³
  - More open grain than white oak
  - Laser cleaning: Lower power than white oak
  
- **English Oak** (Quercus robur)
  - Historical restoration applications
  - Density: 0.72 g/cmÂ³
  
- **Live Oak** (Quercus virginiana)
  - Very dense (0.88 g/cmÂ³)
  - Marine applications

### Maple Variations
- **Hard Maple / Sugar Maple** (Acer saccharum)
  - Density: 0.63-0.75 g/cmÂ³
  - Applications: Flooring, furniture, instruments
  - Laser cleaning: High power due to density
  
- **Soft Maple / Red Maple** (Acer rubrum)
  - Density: 0.54-0.63 g/cmÂ³
  - Easier to clean than hard maple
  
- **Bird's Eye Maple** (figure variation)
  - Same species, different grain pattern
  - Higher value, requires careful cleaning

### Mahogany Variations
- **Genuine Mahogany** (Swietenia macrophylla)
  - Density: 0.50-0.56 g/cmÂ³
  - Applications: Fine furniture, boats
  
- **African Mahogany** (Khaya spp.)
  - Density: 0.42-0.53 g/cmÂ³
  - More variable properties
  
- **Philippine Mahogany** (Shorea spp.) - NOT TRUE MAHOGANY
  - Actually a different genus
  - Different cleaning parameters

### Cherry Variations
- **Black Cherry** (Prunus serotina)
  - Density: 0.56 g/cmÂ³
  - Applications: Cabinets, furniture
  - Darkens with light exposure
  
- **European Cherry** (Prunus avium)
  - Slightly denser
  - Different color characteristics

### Moisture Content Variations
- **Green Wood** (> 30% MC)
  - Higher water absorption
  - Different laser interaction
  
- **Air-Dried** (12-20% MC)
  - Standard for outdoor applications
  
- **Kiln-Dried** (6-10% MC)
  - Standard for indoor furniture
  - Most common for laser cleaning
  
- **Oven-Dried** (0% MC - laboratory reference)

## Stone Materials

### Variation Types
- **Geological varieties** (within same stone type)
- **Quarry origin** (affects composition)
- **Finish** (polished, honed, flamed, bush-hammered)
- **Grade** (Select, Standard, Commercial)
- **Weathering state** (fresh, aged, deteriorated)

### Granite Variations
- **Absolute Black** (Indian)
  - Very fine grain, low porosity
  - Applications: Monuments, countertops
  - Laser cleaning: Excellent results
  
- **Barre Gray** (Vermont, USA)
  - Medium gray, medium grain
  - Historic monuments
  
- **Carnelian** (Minnesota, USA)
  - Red-brown with black specks
  
- **Color variations by mineral content**:
  - White/Light gray: High feldspar
  - Pink/Red: High orthoclase feldspar
  - Black/Dark: High biotite/hornblende
  - Green: Epidote presence

### Marble Variations
- **Carrara** (Tuscany, Italy)
  - White/blue-gray
  - Fine-grained, compact
  - Applications: Sculpture, architecture
  - Laser cleaning: Gentle parameters
  
- **Calacatta** (Italy)
  - Whiter than Carrara, bolder veining
  - Premium grade
  
- **Statuario** (Italy)
  - Purest white
  - Used for famous sculptures
  
- **Emperador** (Spain)
  - Brown with white veining
  - Contains dolomite
  
- **Travertine** (technically limestone, sold as marble)
  - Porous, requires different cleaning

### Limestone Variations
- **Indiana Limestone** (USA)
  - Oolitic structure
  - Grades: Select, Standard, Rustic, Variegated
  - Applications: Buildings, monuments
  
- **Portland Stone** (UK)
  - Historic buildings
  - Relatively soft
  
- **Jura Limestone** (Germany)
  - Beige/gray
  - Fossil inclusions

### Sandstone Variations
- **By grain size**:
  - Fine-grained (< 0.25mm)
  - Medium-grained (0.25-0.5mm)
  - Coarse-grained (> 0.5mm)
  
- **By composition**:
  - Quartz sandstone (high silica)
  - Arkose (high feldspar)
  - Greywacke (dark, mixed composition)

## Composite Materials

### Variation Types
- **Fiber type** (carbon, glass, aramid, natural)
- **Matrix type** (epoxy, polyester, vinyl ester, polyurethane)
- **Fiber orientation** (unidirectional, woven, random)
- **Fiber volume fraction** (30-70%)
- **Manufacturing method** (hand layup, RTM, pultrusion, filament winding)

### Carbon Fiber Composites
- **Standard Modulus (SM) Carbon/Epoxy**
  - Fiber: 230 GPa modulus
  - Matrix: Epoxy resin
  - Applications: Aerospace, automotive
  - Laser cleaning: Risk of matrix damage
  
- **Intermediate Modulus (IM)**
  - 300 GPa modulus
  - Higher cost
  
- **High Modulus (HM)**
  - 400+ GPa modulus
  - Satellite structures
  
- **High Strength (HS)**
  - Optimized for tensile strength
  - Pressure vessels

### Fiberglass (GFRP) Variations
- **E-Glass/Polyester** (Marine Grade)
  - Most economical
  - Applications: Boats, tanks
  - Density: 1.8-2.0 g/cmÂ³
  
- **E-Glass/Epoxy** (Aerospace Grade)
  - Better mechanical properties
  - Higher cost
  
- **S-Glass/Epoxy** (High Strength)
  - 40% stronger than E-glass
  - Military applications
  
- **C-Glass** (Chemical Resistant)
  - Corrosive environments

### Aramid Composites
- **KevlarÂ®49/Epoxy**
  - Applications: Armor, aerospace
  - Excellent impact resistance
  - Laser cleaning: Aramid degrades with UV
  
- **KevlarÂ®29** (Lower modulus)

- **TwaronÂ®** (Teijin)

## Plastic/Polymer Materials

### Variation Types
- **Grade** (virgin, recycled, filled, reinforced)
- **Molecular weight** (affects properties)
- **Additives** (UV stabilizers, flame retardants, colorants)
- **Processing** (injection molded, extruded, cast)
- **Crystallinity** (amorphous vs. semi-crystalline)

### Polyethylene (PE) Variations
- **HDPE** (High Density)
  - Density: 0.93-0.97 g/cmÂ³
  - Higher crystallinity (70-90%)
  - Applications: Bottles, pipes
  
- **LDPE** (Low Density)
  - Density: 0.91-0.93 g/cmÂ³
  - Branched structure
  - Applications: Film, bags
  
- **UHMWPE** (Ultra-High Molecular Weight)
  - MW: 3-6 million
  - Extremely wear resistant
  - Applications: Medical implants, gears

### Polypropylene (PP) Variations
- **Homopolymer PP**
  - Density: 0.90-0.91 g/cmÂ³
  - High stiffness
  
- **Random Copolymer PP**
  - With ethylene
  - Better clarity
  
- **Impact Copolymer PP**
  - With rubber
  - Better low-temperature impact

### Acrylic (PMMA) Variations
- **Cast Acrylic**
  - Better optical properties
  - Applications: Aquariums, signage
  - Laser cleaning: Watch for crazing
  
- **Extruded Acrylic**
  - Lower cost
  - Slightly lower optical quality

### Engineering Plastics
- **Nylon 6** vs **Nylon 6,6**
  - Different polymerization
  - Different water absorption
  
- **PC** (Polycarbonate) variations:
  - UV-stabilized
  - Flame-retardant
  - Glass-filled

## Masonry Materials

### Brick Variations
- **Common/Building Brick**
  - Grade: SW (Severe Weather), MW (Moderate), NW (No Weather)
  - Applications: General construction
  
- **Face Brick**
  - Higher quality finish
  - Color variations: Red, buff, brown, black
  
- **Engineering Brick**
  - Class A: < 4.5% water absorption
  - Class B: < 7% water absorption
  - High compressive strength
  
- **Firebrick/Refractory**
  - High alumina content
  - Kiln/furnace applications
  - Laser cleaning: High power acceptable

### Concrete Variations
- **By strength class**:
  - C20/25 (20 MPa cylinder, 25 MPa cube)
  - C30/37 (standard structural)
  - C40/50 (high strength)
  - C50/60+ (ultra-high strength)
  
- **By aggregate type**:
  - Normal weight (limestone, granite)
  - Lightweight (expanded clay, pumice)
  - Heavy weight (magnetite, barite - radiation shielding)

## Semiconductor Materials

### Silicon Variations
- **Czochralski (CZ) Silicon**
  - Single crystal
  - Oxygen content: 10-20 ppma
  - Applications: Most IC manufacturing
  
- **Float Zone (FZ) Silicon**
  - Ultra-pure (< 1 ppma oxygen)
  - Power electronics
  
- **Silicon-on-Insulator (SOI)**
  - Silicon layer on insulator
  - High-speed devices
  
- **Doping variations**:
  - N-type (phosphorus, arsenic)
  - P-type (boron)
  - Intrinsic (undoped)

### Gallium Arsenide Variations
- **Semi-Insulating GaAs**
  - Applications: RF devices, MMICs
  - Laser cleaning: Very delicate
  
- **N-type GaAs**
  - LEDs, lasers
  
- **P-type GaAs**
  - Solar cells

## Implementation Strategy

### Research Data Structure
For each non-metal variation, populate:

**Material Properties**:
- **density**: Varies by grade/moisture/composition
- **porosity**: Critical for stone/ceramic/wood
- **moistureContent**: Wood-specific
- **hardness**: Mohs scale for minerals, Shore for plastics
- **thermalConductivity**: Affects heat dissipation
- **opticalTransmission**: Glass/transparent materials
- **laserAbsorption**: At relevant wavelengths

**Laser Cleaning Settings**:
- **wavelength**: Optimal varies by material composition
- **powerRange**: Lower for organics, higher for stone/ceramic
- **fluenceThreshold**: Damage threshold varies significantly
- **pulseParameters**: Ns vs ps vs fs for different sensitivities
- **scanSpeed**: Adjusted for porosity and sensitivity

**Variation-Specific Context**:
- **grade**: Commercial, industrial, laboratory
- **condition**: New, aged, weathered, historic
- **finish**: Polished, honed, rough, sealed
- **treatment**: Coated, impregnated, untreated
- **provenance**: Quarry origin, manufacturer

## Key Differences from Metals

| Aspect | Metals (Alloys) | Non-Metals (Variations) |
|--------|----------------|-------------------------|
| **Variation Type** | Alloying elements | Species, grade, treatment, condition |
| **Standards** | AISI, ASTM, AA, ISO | ASTM, ISO, material-specific (wood species, glass types) |
| **Designation** | Numeric (6061-T6) | Descriptive (White Oak, Carrara Marble) |
| **Properties** | Consistent for alloy | Variable within species/grade |
| **Laser Cleaning** | Wavelength selection by metal | Wavelength + power critical, damage risk higher |
| **Moisture/Porosity** | Not applicable | Critical factor |
| **Aging/Weathering** | Oxidation/corrosion | Significant property changes |

## Notes

- Non-metal variations are more complex than metal alloys
- Grade, condition, treatment, and age significantly affect properties
- Laser cleaning parameters much more sensitive to variations
- Moisture content critical for wood and porous materials
- Historic/conservation applications require careful variation documentation
- Standards vary widely (wood species names vs. glass composition codes)
