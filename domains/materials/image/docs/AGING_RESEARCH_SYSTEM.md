## üß¨ Material-Specific Aging Patterns

### **Wood (Hardwood, Softwood, Engineered)**

**Primary Aging Concerns**:
1. **UV Photodegradation** (most visible)
   - Lignin breakdown: color shift white/cream ‚Üí gray ‚Üí silver-gray
   - Cellulose exposure: surface roughening, fiber raising
   - Distribution: UV-facing surfaces, gradient from exposed to shaded
   - Timeline: Visible in weeks (color), months (texture)

2. **Moisture-Induced Degradation**
   - Grain raising, checking, cracking along grain
   - Fungal discoloration (blue stain, brown rot indicators)
   - Water staining: dark streaks following grain
   - Dimensional changes: warping, cupping patterns

3. **Biological Decay**
   - Fungal growth: black/green surface mold
   - Insect damage: exit holes, frass, gallery patterns
   - Bacterial soft rot: surface softening, darkening

4. **Combined Effects**
   - UV + moisture: accelerated fiber degradation
   - Dirt + moisture: biological growth substrate

**Distribution Reality**:
- Grain following: All patterns follow wood grain structure
- End grain concentration: More severe aging at cut ends
- Knot interactions: Different aging around knots (resin protection or weakness)
- Growth ring visibility: Enhanced or obscured by aging

---

### **Metals (Ferrous, Non-Ferrous, Reactive)**

**Primary Aging Concerns**:
1. **Oxidation/Corrosion** (aging as contamination)
   - Iron: Red-brown rust, stratified layers, pitting
   - Copper: Green verdigris, brown cuprite, black patina progression
   - Aluminum: White powdery oxide, pitting corrosion
   - Steel: Surface rust ‚Üí scale ‚Üí pitting ‚Üí structural loss

2. **Galvanic Corrosion** (multi-metal aging)
   - Concentrated at dissimilar metal junctions
   - Electrolyte-dependent patterns (salt spray accelerates)

3. **Stress Corrosion**
   - Cracking at stress concentration points
   - Weld zone corrosion
   - Bend radius accelerated oxidation

**Distribution Reality**:
- Gravity drips: Rust staining below fasteners, joints
- Crevice corrosion: Enhanced at overlaps, gasket interfaces
- Galvanic cells: More severe near dissimilar metals
- Edge concentration: Higher corrosion at cut edges, corners

---

### **Polymers (Thermoplastics, Elastomers, Engineering)**

**Primary Aging Concerns**:
1. **UV Degradation** (photo-oxidation)
   - Color shift: Yellowing, bleaching, chalking
   - Surface chalking: White powdery layer
   - Gloss loss: Glossy ‚Üí matte progression
   - Crazing: Fine surface cracks
   - Timeline: Visible in months (outdoor), years (indoor)

2. **Thermal Oxidation**
   - Embrittlement: Loss of flexibility
   - Discoloration: Yellowing, browning
   - Surface hardening

3. **Chemical Degradation**
   - Hydrolysis: Chain scission from moisture
   - Plasticizer migration: Surface stickiness or brittleness
   - Environmental stress cracking

**Distribution Reality**:
- UV gradient: More severe on sun-facing surfaces
- Stress concentration: Crazing at high-stress points (bends, fasteners)
- Thickness dependent: Thinner sections age faster
- Pigment effects: Dark colors show less discoloration but more heat damage

---

### **Ceramics (Traditional, Glass, Construction)**

**Primary Aging Concerns**:
1. **Weathering/Erosion**
   - Surface roughening from rain, wind, freeze-thaw
   - Efflorescence: White salt deposits from moisture migration
   - Spalling: Surface layer loss from freeze-thaw

2. **Biological Growth**
   - Algae, lichen, moss on moist surfaces
   - Root penetration (plant growth in cracks)
   - Black fungal staining

3. **Chemical Attack**
   - Acid rain etching (glass, marble, limestone)
   - Alkali-silica reaction (concrete)
   - Salt crystallization damage

**Distribution Reality**:
- Moisture accumulation: North-facing (northern hemisphere), shaded zones
- Drainage patterns: Staining along water flow paths
- Porosity concentration: More severe in porous areas
- Sheltering effects: Protected zones age differently

## üîç Distribution Physics Realism

### **Gravity-Driven Patterns**

**Drips & Runs**:
```
High concentration at top
    ‚Üì
Vertical streaks following gravity
    ‚Üì
Accumulation at bottom edge
```

**Pooling**:
- Horizontal surfaces: Uniform coating or evaporation rings
- Depressions: Concentrated contamination/aging
- Edges: Drip lines, overflow patterns

### **Environmental Exposure Gradients**

**UV Exposure**:
```
Direct sun ‚Üí Most severe aging
Indirect light ‚Üí Moderate aging
Shaded ‚Üí Minimal UV aging (but may have moisture issues)
```

**Moisture Exposure**:
```
Constantly wet ‚Üí Biological growth, hydrolysis
Wet-dry cycling ‚Üí Most damaging (expansion/contraction)
Always dry ‚Üí Minimal moisture damage (but UV/oxidation continues)
```

### **Substrate Structure Effects**

**Grain Following** (wood):
- Weathering follows grain orientation
- Cracks form along grain boundaries
- Staining penetrates deeper in end grain

**Stress Concentration** (all materials):
- Cracks initiate at high-stress points
- Bends, holes, notches show accelerated aging
- Weld/joint zones have different corrosion

**Porosity Effects** (porous materials):
- Contamination penetrates deeper in porous zones
- Staining spreads via capillary action
- Drying patterns leave concentration rings

## üß™ Synergistic Effects

### **Aging + Contamination Interactions**

1. **UV + Moisture** (most damaging to organics)
   - UV breaks down surface ‚Üí moisture penetrates deeper
   - Wet-dry cycling with UV: maximum degradation rate

2. **Oil + Heat** (polymers, metals)
   - Thermal oxidation of oil creates thick deposits
   - Oil traps heat, accelerating underlying material aging

3. **Biological + Chemical** (ceramics, wood)
   - Organism byproducts etch surfaces
   - Root penetration creates moisture pathways

4. **Salt + UV** (metals, polymers)
   - Salt accelerates corrosion
   - UV + salt spray: extreme degradation

### **Protective Interactions**

1. **Patina Formation** (copper, bronze)
   - Initial corrosion protects from further degradation
   - Stable oxide layer slows continued aging

2. **Surface Hardening** (some polymers)
   - UV-induced cross-linking creates protective skin
   - Prevents deeper penetration (but surface becomes brittle)
