```markdown
## Overview  
Zebrawood is a dense hardwood known for its distinctive grain pattern, making it a popular choice for decorative and functional applications. It is particularly relevant for laser cleaning due to its moderate porosity, low to moderate reflectivity at 1064 nm, and thermal properties. With an ignition point of approximately 300°C, careful laser parameter selection is required to avoid damage. Its composition of cellulose, lignin, hemicellulose, and extractives contributes to its structural stability and interaction with laser systems.

## Properties  
- **Density**: 790-850 kg/m³  
- **Hardness**: 1570 lbf (Janka hardness)  
- **Thermal Conductivity**: 0.12-0.15 W/m·K  
- **Ignition Point**: ~300°C  
- **Porosity**: Moderate (varies with grain structure)  
- **Reflectivity**: Low to moderate for 1064 nm, higher for UV wavelengths  
- **Moisture Content**: 8-12% (equilibrium at 20°C, 65% RH)  
- **Young's Modulus**: 10-12 GPa  

## Composition  
Zebrawood consists of the following structural components:  
- **Cellulose**: 40-50%  
- **Lignin**: 20-30%  
- **Hemicellulose**: 15-25%  
- **Extractives**: 3-5%  

## Laser Parameters  
For laser cleaning applications, the following parameters are recommended:  
- **Wavelength**: 1064-1070 nm (Nd:YAG) or 10.6 µm (CO₂)  
- **Pulse Duration**: 10-100 ns (Q-switched)  
- **Energy Density**: 0.5-2.5 J/cm² (adjust based on soiling level)  
- **Scanning Speed**: 500-2000 mm/s (dependent on spot size)  

## Environmental Considerations  
- **Sustainability**: Vulnerable in some regions; verify sustainable sourcing.  
- **Recycling Potential**: High (reusable as lumber or downcycled to particleboard).  
- **Processing Impact**: Low if harvested sustainably.  
- **Hazardous Byproducts**: Fine wood dust and potential VOCs from extractives.  

## Compatibility  
Zebrawood is compatible with the following materials for finishing and stabilization:  
- **Wood Stains**: Suitable for surface finishing.  
- **Epoxy Resins**: Used for stabilization and repair.  
- **UV-Cured Coatings**: Applied as protective layers.  

## Regulatory Standards  
- **CITES Appendix III**: Regulates trade for certain origins.  
- **FSC**: Certifies sustainable forestry practices.  
- **ASTM D143**: Standard test methods for small clear wood specimens.
```
