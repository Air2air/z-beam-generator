```markdown
## Overview  
Maple wood is a dense and hard material commonly used in woodworking and surface finishing applications. With a density ranging from 600-750 kg/m³ and a Janka hardness of 1450 lbf, it is a durable choice for various industrial uses. Its medium to high porosity and low to moderate reflectivity make it suitable for laser cleaning, particularly due to its high absorptivity for IR wavelengths (e.g., 1064 nm). Maple wood is also compatible with adhesives, varnishes, and stains, making it versatile for finishing processes.  

## Properties  
- **Density:** 600-750 kg/m³  
- **Hardness (Janka):** 1450 lbf  
- **Thermal Conductivity:** 0.16 W/m·K  
- **Ignition Point:** 300-400 °C  
- **Porosity:** Medium to high  
- **Reflectivity:** Low to moderate (varies with wavelength)  
- **Moisture Content:** 6-12% (equilibrium moisture content)  
- **Young's Modulus:** 10-12 GPa  
- **Absorptivity:** High for IR wavelengths (e.g., 1064 nm)  

## Composition  
Maple wood consists of the following structural components:  
- **Cellulose:** 40-50%  
- **Hemicellulose:** 20-30%  
- **Lignin:** 20-25%  
- **Extractives:** 1-5%  

## Compatibility  
Maple wood is compatible with:  
- **Adhesives** for woodworking  
- **Varnishes** for surface finishing  
- **Stains** for coloring  
- **Laser cleaning** for surface preparation  

## Laser Parameters  
For laser cleaning applications, the following parameters are recommended:  
- **Wavelength:** 1064 nm (IR), 532 nm (green)  
- **Pulse Duration:** 10-100 ns  
- **Energy Density:** 0.5-3 J/cm²  
- **Scanning Speed:** 100-500 mm/s  

## Environmental Considerations  
- **Sustainability:** Renewable resource if sustainably harvested  
- **Recycling Potential:** High (biodegradable, reusable)  
- **Processing Impact:** Low if processed without harmful chemicals  
- **Hazardous Byproducts:** Smoke and particulates (containing VOCs and PAHs)  

## Regulatory Standards  
Maple wood adheres to the following standards:  
- **ASTM D143:** Standard test methods for small clear specimens of timber  
- **EN 350:** Durability of wood and wood-based products  
- **FSC:** Forest Stewardship Council certification for sustainable wood
```
