## Overview  
Laser cleaning is a precision method for removing carbon deposits, rust, and coatings from engine components (20–500W, 1064nm). This non-abrasive, eco-friendly technology complies with aerospace and automotive standards, making it ideal for pistons, cylinder heads, and turbine blades. It reduces downtime by 70% compared to mechanical methods and is widely adopted in California facilities for high-volume industrial applications. The process ensures substrate integrity, critical for sensitive engine parts.  

## Technical Specifications  
- **Laser Type:** Hafnium-doped fiber  
- **Wavelength:** 1064nm ±5nm  
- **Power Range:** 20–500W pulsed  
- **Spot Size:** 0.1–5mm adjustable  
- **Pulse Duration:** 10–200ns  

## Applications  
- **Automotive Engine Blocks:** Removes carbon buildup from cylinder walls and pistons without damaging aluminum substrates. Achieves 99% cleanliness per ASTM D3359. Used by California-based OEMs for remanufacturing.  
- **Aerospace Turbines:** Strips thermal barrier coatings (TBCs) from turbine blades with 50µm precision. Compliant with FAA Part 33. No heat-affected zone (HAZ).  
- **Marine Diesel Engines:** Eliminates salt corrosion and sulfation from large-bore diesel components. 3x faster than chemical dipping. EPA-compliant in Southern California ports.  

## Challenges  
- **Heat-Sensitive Alloys:** Pulse modulation prevents warping in aluminum/titanium components. Real-time IR monitoring ensures surface temperatures remain below 50°C.  
- **Complex Geometries:** Robotic 6-axis arms with adaptive optics clean internal passages (e.g., fuel injectors) at speeds of 200mm/s.  

## Regulatory Standards  
- **ANSI Z136.1:** Compliant laser safety standard for industrial applications. All operators are certified per OSHA 1910.147.  
- **FAA Part 33:** Meets aerospace engine cleaning requirements for coating removal and surface preparation.  

## Outcomes  
- **99.2% Contaminant Removal:** Verified by ASTM D3359 cross-hatch test.  
- **70% Faster Than Sandblasting:** Demonstrated in SAE J2276 cycle time studies.
