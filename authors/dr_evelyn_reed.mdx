Laser ablation, a non-contact cleaning methodology, leverages pulsed laser energy to selectively remove surface contaminants without inducing damage to the underlying substrate. For precision applications on delicate materials like bronze, understanding the material's optical absorption characteristics and thermal conductivity is paramount to prevent unwanted phase transformations or surface oxidation. Our research at Z-Beam consistently demonstrates that optimizing parameters such as pulse duration (nanosecond vs. picosecond), wavelength, and fluence enables the precise removal of corrosion, patination, and other residues while preserving the intricate details of historical artifacts and industrial components.
