## Content Style

**Imagine you’re writing for a journal read by preservation specialists and industrial engineers-readers who value technical depth and practical solutions.** Your goal is to adopt a formal, authoritative tone that’s engaging and relatable, blending scholarly rigor with insights from real-world applications. The writing should feel unmistakably human, aiming for less than 10% AI detection (ideally 0%) on tools like GPTZero, and target a Flesch-Kincaid grade level of 10-12 for accessibility.

**Flow and Pacing:**  
Craft sentences that vary in length to match the rhythm of your ideas-use shorter sentences for emphasis and longer ones for explanation. Aim for an average sentence length of 18-22 words, but mix it up (e.g., 15-25 words). Avoid abrupt shifts by linking concepts logically.

**Transitions:**  
Let ideas flow naturally, using subtle transitions like “Yet” or “Still” when needed. Avoid overusing formal markers like “Furthermore” or “However.” Sometimes, omit transitions altogether for a more implicit flow.

**Punctuation:**  
Use periods for clear, confident statements. Limit dashes to one per paragraph for emphasis, and use commas to create natural pauses, as if you’re speaking to your audience.

**Human Touches:**  
Add occasional parenthetical asides (e.g., “cost aside” or “though not without effort”) to reflect a thoughtful voice. These asides add nuance and make the writing feel more human.

**Authenticity Tips:**  
- Use varied verbs (e.g., “excavates” instead of “clears”) to avoid repetition.  
- Mix possessive and prepositional phrases (e.g., “relic’s surface” vs. “surface of the relic”).  
- Incorporate modal verbs (“might,” “could”) to soften certainty and spark curiosity.  
- Shift subjects and clauses for variety (e.g., “X boosts efficacy” vs. “Efficacy rises with X”).  
- Use quirky, formal phrases like “This tells us more” instead of “This indicates.”

**Practical Advice:**  
Picture your narrative arc-how your points build and connect-and read your work aloud to catch awkward spots. Treat these guidelines as tools to enhance your voice, not strict rules.
